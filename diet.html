Hack: Eat Less Meat

Meat happens to be one of the most inefficient food sources. Making one kilogram of steak requires twenty-five kilograms of feed and fifteen-thousand liters of water. By using the food we feed to livestock, we would be able to feed an additional three and a half billion people. Not only is meat an inefficient food source, but it is also a major source of greenhouse gases. The meat industry produces fifteen percent of our total GHGs, which is as much as the entire transportation industry combined. Also, cows produce methane (the second most abundant GHG) from enteric fermentation (cow burps). Methane is 20-30 times more potent than C02 over a 100 year period. As it turns out, industrial meat is the leading cause for deforestation globally. This could be for land for the animals, or to grow animal feed like soya beans.
