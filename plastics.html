Hack: Avoid Single-Use Plastics

According to a report from Greenpeace on the state of plastic recycling in the US, the amount of plastic being recycled into something new has fallen to new lows of around five percent. They found that not plastic met the standard to be called "recycleable" (set by the Ellen MacArthur Foundation New Plastic Economy Initiative). To meet this, plastic must be recycled at a rate of thirty percent, which has never happened. This plastic recycling problem only continues to get worse, meaning that to manage this crisis we must make massive changes. The plastic industry is set to triple its plastic production by 2050. The main problem with plastic is that it is expensive to collect and sort. At this point, there are thousands of different plastics and they cannot be melted together. Greenpeace also found that plastic degrades after one or two uses, and the more time its reused the more toxic it becomes.
